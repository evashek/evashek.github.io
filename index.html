LEAF

Text-based adventure game written in Lisp with three other students. The player's goal is to get themself (a young leaf) to the Tree of Knowledge by heading to different towns where leaves will pose a question they must answer to move on. Getting an answer wrong will not immediately cause them to fail the game, as their progress determines whether they continue on a "win" or "lose" path. I was in charge of writing the function where the character moves between towns.

Used: Common Lisp, LispWorks

1-Mile

1-Mile was born from my participation in Startup Weekend 2013 hosted by the university. The purpose of the event was to try to launch a startup business with your team within a weekends time by preparing a business plan and presentation. Although I did not take part in programming the application, I helped conceptualize, write a business plan, and apply my research and field work results to make improvements. The application was meant to be location-based broadcasting app where only people within one mile radius of you will be able to see what you have posted. The more "likes" a post gains, the bigger the radius.

Surferpedia

As the name suggests, Surferpedia is meant to be an encyclopedia of surfers that grows based on user contribution (like Wikipedia). The project had several iterations starting from the middle of the semester such as first building the initial site, creating a database, adding the ability to register for a new account, and upgrading text input into selection menus. The final test, in a group of two, was to include one extra feature. I chose to create a user profile page which reported the user's contributions and viewing history.

Dive Planner

A dive planner is a helpful tool which divers use to outline their next dive as safely as possible given the effects of diving such as nitrogen buildup in one's bloodstream. Most of the time, this is done manually by learning how to read a diving table (which is quite complicated) and doing calculations. The dive planner web app is essentially this diving table, but will calculate and show only the safe depths or times to dive/rest. My participation involved front end development and inputing dive table data into the database.

Used: HTML, CSS, XAMPP (Apache, MySQL), PHP, Google Charts API, Git
